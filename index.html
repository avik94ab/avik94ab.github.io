About

My research interests include immunogenetics and structural immunoinformatics. I earned a B.Tech in Applied Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering from West Bengal University of Technology, India in 2015. Subsequently, I pursued a Master of Engineering (M.E) in Biomedical Engineering from Jadavpur University, India in 2017. In 2023, I achieved a significant milestone by completing a PhD in Computer Science from Tulane University, focusing on the use of structural bioinformatics for CD4+ T-cell epitope prediction. This research has made substantial contributions to understanding and predicting critical components of the immune system. Currently, as a Postdoctoral Scholar at the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF), my research is laser-focused on the immunogenetics of neurological disorders, particularly emphasizing multiple sclerosis. I am driven by the aspiration to deepen our understanding of these disorders, ultimately contributing to improved diagnostics and treatment strategies.
